Races

Races

Human

Lowest XP penalty. Lowest lifespan (100). No natural resistances. Has access to every Guild - join all the things.

Giant

Highest Strength (41). Medium natural resists. No spellcasting. Best Warrior.

Saris (half-elf)

Highest Dexterity. High Mental/Stone/Acid resistances. Best Thief.

Elf

Highest Intelligence & Wisdom. High Magic resist. Excellent Spellcasters. Best Sorcerer/Warlock/Cleric.

Gnome

Highest Charisma. High Mind, Magic & Electricity resistances. Very good Spellcasters. Best Magi. Good Paladin.

Dwarf

Good Strength and Constitution. High Drain, Medium Disease/Poison resistances. Strong as Warrior/Cleric and/or Explorer/Thief.

Troll

Above average Strength (34). No spellcasting. Best pure Ninja.

Ogre

Highest Con (30). High resistances. Worse Giant.

Yeti

Best Breathing. Avoid!

Guilds

There are broadly two types of guilds: Martial and Spellcaster

Martial guilds focus on melee combat and typically learn a secondary skill such as Fighting or Thieving or basic magic.

Spellcaster guilds grant access to a wide range of spells including Healing, Blasting, Movement, Resistance and Charming. However, Spellcaster guilds require approximately twice as much experience to level than martial guilds and provide much less Attack/Defence than any martial guild. On top of this, they generally suffer greater experience penalties than melee classes when multi-classing due to the high penalties imposed by A/D and Multiple Swings of martial guilds.

If planning on joining a spellcasting guild, I strongly advise that character take 30 levels of Artisan first. Artisan 30 provides high hit points, excellent A/D and good access to items - three things spellcasters desperately need.

Multiclassing and Experience Penalties

A character may be a member of as many guilds as their race permits.

When a character is a member of more than one guild, each guild applies an experience penalty to the all the other guild(s) that they are a member of, based on the abilities learned from that guild.

Penalties largely come from abilities: Multiple Swings, Fighting, Thieving, Perception, Language, Backstab and Critical Hit. Spells and Spell Power do not apply penalties to other guilds. Spellcasting guilds tend to give very small penalties to other guilds and suffer from high penalties from other guilds.

The severity of the penalty is based on how powerful the abilities provided by a guild are, relative to your total abilities. Guilds such as Ninja & Villain contribute skills in a wide variety of areas, so tend to suffer less from penalties, but inflict large penalties on other guilds.

Penalties maximise at 1.5, which requires a character to get 150% extra experience, or 2.5 times the total experience per level.

Penalties are applied retroactively.A level 100 Warlock joins the Warrior’s guild and earns levels in Warrior. This will apply penalty experience to the Warlock’s next level for all previous 100 Warlock levels, each time the Warrior gains a level. This is also sometimes referred to as ‘idle’ experience.
