Getting started

Intro

Nioh 2 is one of my favourite games of all time. It has an interesting story, based on characters and events from the Sengoku period of 16th century Japan in a short of alternate history. with deep and complex combat and itemisation systems. With completion of NG+4 and several hundred hours playtime, I believe I have a reasonable understanding of the game mechanics.

In my quest to understand the underlining mechanics in this game, I have found that there is a lot of conflicting and/or missing information online. Perhaps due to updates and DLC. So, I’ve compiled this guide that is up to date as of Nioh 2 - Complete Edition, version 1.28.8 on PC (which is likely to be the final version). It covers New Game - the first playthrough - all the way through NG+++ and the Underworld (endgame).

This guide aims to equip you with all the knowledge that you need to be effective at Nioh 2 without necessarily holding your hand and telling you exactly what to do in each part of the game. I will forgo explaining basic mechanics of the game as the game does a well enough job of teaching these to you as you play. I will aim to give recommendations as to which things to choose and invest in for the smoothest experience as there is much to pick from. In general, you aren’t ever locked out of anything skill-wise and re-training your stats or skill choices is relatively cheap and easy to do at any point in the game.

Weapons

Melee Weapons

One of the first choices you will need to make after character creation is what melee weapon(s) you will use. You can hold two weapons at once and can freely switch between both weapons on the fly and even completely change weapon types as any time you choose thought the game with no penalty other than needing to gain experience with the new weapon type.

I encourage you to try out as many weapons as you can to get a feel for which style suites you the most, as they all play a little differently. Each weapon has its own skill tree and unique attack skills and even hidden skills that can be found later in the game. Slower weapons are more suited to being pared with heavier armours for more Toughness (more on this later), while others are faster and favour using lighter amours for better Ki management while dodging more to avoid attacks.

The game lets you equip two different types (or same type) of weapons at once. I encourage sticking to a single weapon type as the combat system is so deep and complex that learning two weapons initially can be overwhelming. As you play through the game, you may find that your second weapon will often end up being unused in combat and only equipped for a set bonus. This is the simplest way to play and works just fine.

Ranged Weapons

In addition to two melee weapons, you can equip two ranged weapons. Unlike melee weapons, I encourage using two different types of ranged weapons. Ammo is fairly limited and by using two different types you will effectively double your ammo capacity between shrines. I recommend using the combination of a Bow for easy enemies and Musket for enemies with helmets as it pierces helmets where the bow does not. Shooting 'unaware' enemies will grant a massive damage bonus and often one-shot most if hit in the head/weak point. Do this as often as you can.

Damage Types

Damage Types and Debuffs

As you progress you will find Electricity, Water and Fire elemental effects on weapons and spells. I recommend choosing either Purity or Corruption damage over the elemental types. No enemies resist either of these types and the debuffs they inflict are very strong.

Purity

The Purity status effect increases the Ki damage taken by 50% and prevents any new buffs from being applied. This is great for Onmyo-magic using human enemies as it stops them healing and using barriers/buffs on themselves. The purity debuff will make an enemy take a lot of Ki damage and draining all of a human enemies Ki will open them up for a Finisher. This is good. Finishers do a lot of damage.

Corruption

Corruption on the other hand increases Ki damage taken by 30% and you steal a portion of the Ki damage done to enemies that have this debuff. Additionally, corruption reduces the maximum Ki of Yokai enemies. If the maximum Ki reaches zero, the Yokai will become stunned for a short period. This is very effective against Yokai enemies and Yokai bosses.

Confusion

Combining any two debuffs (Fire/Water/Electricity/Purity/Corruption) will cause the enemy to gain Confusion. This increases all damage taken by 50%. This is multiplicative and one of the highest damage increases in the entire game. This is especially effective against bosses who have significant health pools but also very useful against tougher enemies. Note that Purity and corruption are mutually exclusive: you cannot apply both Purity and Corruption at the same time, but either of these can be combined with Fire, Water or Electricity debuffs to apply Confusion.

Purity is great against Human enemies while Corruption is effective against Yokai enemies.

Onmyo and Ninjitsu

Onmyo Magic

Magic is very powerful in Nioh 2, in especially the self-buffs that heal and support your character. Magic also works well to provide a second elemental status in addition to your primary weapon for the potent Confusion debuff. I strongly suggest that every character raise the Magic stat (along with ever other stat) to 10 and use Omnyo Magic Talismans.

Talismans are prepared at any Shrine where you can prepare any number of talismans up to your total capacity, or the maximum capacity of the talisman. A higher Magic stat equals more total capacity, capping at 30 magic). The more talismans you prepare, the more uses you will have and resting at a shine restores all uses.

I strongly suggest using Barrier Talisman, and if capacity allows: Rejuvenation and/or Extraction Talisman. Barrier provides massive Ki regen, Rejuvenation provides life regen – very useful for countering poison – and Extraction Talisman. Extraction, when combined with Life Recovery (Amrita Absorption), Increased Attack and Increased Defence provide easy access to very useful buffs. All of these talismans are useful for the entire game and work well with any weapon.

Ninjitsu

Just like Onmyo magic, Ninjitsu is very useful for any character, providing easy ranged damage and strong buffs. It is prepared and used in the same way as Omnyo Magic. By far, the most useful of these being Gunpowder Bomb for easily dealing with enemies that are resistant to physical weapon attacks.

End Game

New Game Plus (NG+)

Each new game cycle has a few new mechanics, higher level enemies with new moves or new enemies added. Ranged enemies fire significantly faster. The most impactful change is the extra damage that you will receive from enemies, so you will want to start thinking about specialising in a particular weapon and unlocking any available Mystic Arts for your weapon, Ninjitsu and Magic. Look for equipment upgrades as you go.

One of the fastest ways to find upgraded equipment is to kill Revenants early in each game cycle. These will drop high level and upgraded equipment equivalent to the level you would find at the end of the cycle, making the whole cycle easier. They can be difficult to defeat, especially at the start of each cycle, but worth the rewards. Summon Benevolent Spirit assistance if needed as summons won’t impact the rewards.

The fifth cycle, Dream of the Nioh, is entirely optional and so my advice is to skip it entirely and head straight to the Underworld, which is accessible from the start of Dream of the Nioh. It will be quite challenging at the start, but the Amrita earned, and equipment found from the Underworld is so much better than what you will have that it is absolutely worth doing right away.

The Underworld

Upon completion of NG+++ Dream of the Wise, the Underworld will become accessible. This is the ‘end game’ area, consisting of 108 floors. Each floor is fixed in layout, consisting of two parts: the Netherscape (main area) and the Crucible (boss arena). There are always 4 Kodama hidden throughout each Netherscape, that provide buffs when in that floors Crucible.

Completing a floor’s boss allows you to either continue or progress to the next floor. Progressing this way gives you a chance to enter a Treasure Room instead of the normal floor.

Clearing Underworld floor 108 grants access to the Depths. These final 30 floors are of significantly higher difficulty, and unlike the underworld are randomly generated.

Ethereal Graces

Ethereal Graces

Starting from Dream of the Wise (the fourth new game cycle) Ethereal Graces will start to drop. These are stronger than Divine Graces and are always of Ethereal quality. The most consistent place to obtain graces is in the Underworld. Ethereal graces aren't at all needed before this and I would not recommend farming them before entering. Use Divine graces or sets for the normal NG+ cycles.

Each floor of the Underworld will drop Ethereal Graces from one of the below drop pools, depending on the floor. This starts at p1 on floor 1 then p2 on floor 2 and p3 on floor 3. The cycle then restarts at p1 on floor 4 and continues in that way.

There are two Ethereal Graces, only found in the Depths floors 6-30. These are Grace of Shinatsuhiko and Grace of Oyamatsumi.
